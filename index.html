About Me

I'm a teacher, a learner, and a problem-solver.
↓  

Work Experience

  ↓

I currently work part-time as a Teacher of Digital Technologies. I help kids understand some of the basic ideas behind writing code using Scratch (block-coding), and also teach them about robots as IPO (input-process-output) systems, using Lego EV3 Mindstorms as an example for prototyping robots for specific tasks.

Throughout my teaching career I have also worked as a Teacher of High School Mathematics, and as a Teacher of Intermediate (middle-school) Sciences (including Chemistry, Physics, Earth Sciences, and Biology).

Prior to beginning my teaching career I worked in hospitality and retail positions, doing tasks such as serving customers, training new-hires, stocktake and ordering, cleaning, food and drink preparation (espresso coffees, fast-foods, and delicatessen foods), merchandising, and operating a variety of POS systems and cash-registers.

Study & Learning Experience

  ↓

At present I study part-time using Codecademy to learn skills required to be a Full-Stack Engineer. I began in July of 2022, and so far I have learnt to use HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Git. I have also begun a course about electricity and electronics, which I have put on hold while I complete this Codecademy Course

I have completed a short online course about block-coding using Scratch when I began my position as a Teacher of Digital Technologies.

Before teaching, I completed my Bachelor's Degree in Science (B.Sc.) at Massey University, followed by a Graduate Diploma in Teaching - Primary (Grad.Dip.Teac-Primary) which was offered at the same campus.

At high-school, my priorities and abilities centered around Maths and Visual Arts, but I completed Levels 1, 2, and 3 of NCEA in a variety of other subjects as well.

Other Hobbies

  ↓

Gardening is my most recent and flourishing passion. I began with an interest in growing my own vegetables and herbs, and have since become obsessed with a variety of foliage and flowering plants and the ecological impacts they have on local environments and wildlife.

I learnt to knit when my Grandma's arthritis progressed to the point that she was no longer able to. I wanted to help others with knitting in the same way that she did - by knitting for babies and donating to hopsitals and other people that could make use of the items. I have learned the skill, but I am still not fast or confident enough to produce the quality or quantity of garments that I would need to.

Crochet is a long-term hobby that I began in my teens. I have created a myriad of items, including soft-toys, blankets, cloths, baskets, and baby-clothing.

As I mentioned earlier, I thoroughly enjoyed visual arts at high-school. Namely drawing with pencil and painting with acrylics. Post high-school, I even designed a few tattoos (mainly just for myself though).
